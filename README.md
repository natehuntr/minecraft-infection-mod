# minecraft-infection-mod

This project is a STEM-focused, systems-based learning initiative implemented as a
modular add-on to the video game Minecraft, one of the most widely used interactive
digital platforms globally. Its goal is to improve intuitive understanding of cause-and-
effect relationships in disease prevention and health systems—specifically timing, partial
protection, waning effects, tradeoffs, access, and context dependence—without
persuasion, messaging, or advocacy.

Motivated by real-world experience in disease prevention, the project recognizes that
scientific efficacy alone does not guarantee population-level impact. Uptake,
persistence, access, trust, and fit within daily life strongly shape outcomes. These are
not individual failures but system-level phenomena spanning individual, community, and
population dynamics.

Rather than asking what is “wrong” with people, the project asks why people may not
access, accept, want, or be able to use prevention tools, and how systems shape those
outcomes. By embedding prevention dynamics as background game mechanics, players
experience how systems behave over time, reinforcing the connection between
individual choices and collective outcomes. 

The mod will involve one or multiple infectious diseases, with fictional names, that are able to spread via a range of vectors and differ in their severity, virulence, and ability to be adequated protected against with one or multiple vaccinations.
